AI CCTV Heatmap Analytics

SmartCounter transforms your CCTV cameras into intelligent heatmap generators. See exactly where customers go, how long they stay, and which store areas need attention — all powered by AI computer vision.

[Heatmap Dashboard Screenshot — Floor Plan with Heat Overlay]

How It Works

SmartCounter's heatmap engine processes millions of coordinate data points from overhead sensors and renders them as intuitive color-coded overlays on your actual store floor plan. Red zones indicate high traffic, blue zones indicate low traffic, and everything in between is precisely calibrated.

Unlike manual observation, heatmaps capture every movement, every hour of the day, across weeks and months. You can compare peak vs off-peak patterns side by side, identify dead zones, and measure the impact of layout changes with actual data.

Key Capabilities

  • Real-time heatmap overlay on your uploaded store floor plan
  • Historical comparison — compare any two time periods side by side
  • Zone-level drill down — select any area for detailed metrics
  • Automatic hot zone and dead zone detection with alerts
  • Export heatmap images as high-resolution PNG for presentations
  • Multi-location comparison — see heatmaps across all your stores

Why Heatmaps Matter for Retail

Retail space is expensive. Every square meter needs to earn its keep. Without spatial data, you're relying on gut feeling to decide product placement, promotional displays, and store layout.

"After analyzing our heatmap data, we moved the accessories display from a dead zone near the back wall to the high-traffic corridor between fitting rooms and checkout. Accessory sales increased 34% in the first month."

Common Use Cases

  • Optimize product placement by positioning high-margin items in hot zones
  • Evaluate promotional display effectiveness by measuring foot traffic before and after
  • Redesign store layout based on natural customer flow patterns
  • Identify underperforming areas and test interventions with A/B comparisons

Technical Details

Heatmaps are generated from track position data collected by overhead sensors at sub-second intervals. The rendering engine uses kernel density estimation (KDE) with configurable bandwidth, producing smooth gradient overlays at up to 4K resolution. Data is processed on our cloud pipeline — no local GPU required.

Floor plans are uploaded once through the admin panel (supported formats: PNG, JPG, SVG). Sensor positions are calibrated during installation, mapping pixel coordinates to physical space. Recalibration is rarely needed after initial setup.
